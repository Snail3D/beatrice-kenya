Yot kamano nonro ma iwinjo

A few words for you to hear first

Beatrice yweyo diriso mar chiemo oda matin mage e Koririsaang' ma Podho. Beatrice is opening a window restaurant from her little house in Western Kenya.

Ok en kabeda mar mabeyo. En diriso, kabuor, kich, keding'a, kod min ma yie timo chiemo ne ji. It is not a fancy place. It is a window, a counter, a stove, a kettle, and a mother who knows how to feed people.

Lweny ariyo maduong' neno kotel mage pile: jopuo ma oting'o ma oyudo chiemo motelo kapok ne somo, kendo jotich e ospital ma oyudo chiemo muop apakna molimo. Gibiro duogo ka chiemo ni ber kendo nengo ni maber. Ma en tich gi. Two big crowds walk past her gate every single day: the college students who need a hot plate before class, and the hospital workers who need a fast meal on a long shift. They will come back when the food is good and the price is fair. That is the whole business.

"Chakula maber, pile maber, thuolo maber. Ka iyie winjo, iyawo biro."
Good food, good welcome, good measure. When you hear about it, they will come.

Rieko ni piny: tim chiemo abich kata auchiel ber ahinya. Los M-Pesa till. Yweyo WhatsApp. Miyo nesi chiemo ma ok ochiw pile. Wak jo puo mondo owang' wach. Kuom dwe achiel, diriso bi yie. The plan is simple: cook five or six things very well. Set up an M-Pesa till. Open WhatsApp. Hand out a few free plates to the nurses. Tell the students to spread the word. Within one month, the window will be known.

Wach motelo: namba mar till en ne jopayo, ok ne iyie. En namba ma gibiro oroe, ok ma iyudo. Ranyisi matin ma en gima weng'o gedo kod gima tich ma opogore giyo.
Tip: the till number is for customers, not for you. It is their number to send to, not for you to receive. That small detail is what makes the difference between a professional and a hobby.

1

Puo mar Nengo mar Chiemo

Food Cost Percentage

Nyagli moromo amora ok onego oyudi it malo e 35 percent mar gima inywalo. Ka nyagli mari ni oyudo chuwo moloyo ma, itiyo ne chiemo, ok ne chiemo tiyo ne iyie. Every dish should cost you no more than 35 percent of what you sell it for. If your plate costs more than that to make, you are working for the food, not the food working for you.

food cost % = (ingredients cost / selling price) × 100
Nonro: Kuon kod rech. Rech (200 g) en KSh 120. Unga (100 g) en KSh 12. Marge kod ombulu en KSh 15. Chuwo duto: KSh 147.
Inywalo nyagli ne KSh 250. Ma: (147 / 250) × 100 = 58.8 percent.
Nyagli ma ni chuwo matek. Ket nengo ne KSh 450, kata ng'i rech matin, kata kelo kichni. Ok inyalo weyo kaka kamano.
Example: Ugali and fish. The fish (200 g) is KSh 120. The ugali flour (100 g) is KSh 12. The oil and salt is KSh 15. Total ingredients: KSh 147. You sell the plate for KSh 250. So: (147 / 250) × 100 = 58.8 percent. This plate is too expensive to make. Either raise the price to KSh 450, or buy smaller fish, or reduce the portion. Do not just accept it.

Nengo maber ne diriso en 25-35 percent. Gima malo 40 nyalo seyo ni chuwo mari oyie kendo chieng' achiel ma ooyo bi ketho juma moromo gi tich.
The healthier number for a window restaurant is 25 to 35 percent. Anything above 40 means your profit is too thin and one bad day will wipe out a week of work.

Nengo mar Pile mar Wuoyo

Daily Break-Even

Nyagli adi ma onego unywalo pile kapok in to chuwo, ok gima kelo chuwo kende. Ma en namba ma nyiso seche ma podho timo chiemo kod seche ma onego iling'. How many plates do you need to sell each day before you start making a profit, not just covering costs. This is the number that tells you when to keep cooking and when to close early.

break-even plates = daily fixed costs / average profit per plate
Nonro: Chuwo mo piny pile (seko mar oda, magadi, kerosine, pi, simu, M-Pesa) = KSh 600. Chuwo moyudore mar nyagli achiel (nengo mar chuwo miyo chal chal) = KSh 120.
Wuoyo: 600 / 120 = nyagli 5 pile ma kelo chuwo kende.
Nyagli moromo amora kuom nyagli 5 en pesa e pokomari. Nyagli 25 en chuwo ma oluwo. Nyagli 40 en chieng' maber.
Example: Your fixed costs each day (rent share, charcoal, kerosene, water, phone, M-Pesa fees) = KSh 600. Your average profit per plate (selling price minus ingredients minus gas) = KSh 120. Break-even: 600 / 120 = 5 plates per day just to cover your costs. Every plate after plate number 5 is money in your pocket. Plate number 25 is real profit. Plate number 40 is a good day.

Ne diriso e Koririsaang' ma Podho, nyagli 25-40 pile en juma maber mar tich. Matino 15 nyiso ni ilos pesa kendo onego ilok nengo kata nono jopayo mang'eny.
For a Western Kenya window restaurant, 25 to 40 plates per day is a healthy working week. Less than 15 means you are losing money and need to adjust prices or find more customers.

Seche mar Wuoyo

Reorder Point

Inyalo weyo rech gungo e Jumapil. Inyalo weyo unga gungo okinyi Jumapil. Puo ma nyiso seche ma onego idhi e maket. Never run out of fish on a Friday. Never run out of ugali flour on a Saturday morning. This formula tells you when to walk to the market.

reorder point = (average daily usage × lead time in days) + safety stock
Nonro: Itiyo kod rech 2 kg pile. Maket en chieng' ariyo molimo (nino kendo Jumapil, maket en Wuoktich). Ihera chwiri ma kelo odhiambo 1 chieng' mondo ok unyalo gungo.
Wuoyo: (2 × 2) + 2 = 6 kg ma odong'.
Ka rech mari odong' e 6 kg, or jomoko dhi maket okinyi ma oluwo. WUOYO E 6 KG
Example: You use about 2 kg of fish per day. The market is 2 days away (today and Saturday, market day is Monday). You want a 1-day safety buffer so you do not run dry. Reorder point: (2 × 2) + 2 = 6 kg remaining. When your fish gets down to 6 kg, send someone to the market the next morning. REORDER AT 6 KG

Seche ma yudo timo godo namba mar chieng' koa kuom sa ma iyie ni in kod kod sa ma oyudo e kichni. E Koririsaang' ma Podho, ma en chieng' 1-3 kata ka gimoro opogore kod yore mag loyo.
Lead time means how many days between realizing you need something and having it in your kitchen. In rural Kenya, that is usually 1 to 3 days depending on the item and your transport.

Pogop Chuwogo

Profit Split

Ranyisi ma piny ma kelo tich tich kendo oda podho chiemo kuom kinde kaka kamano. Inyalo los gik ma. Sheleng' moromo amora ma donjo ni tich kapok otim gik. A simple rule that keeps the business alive and the household fed at the same time. Do not skip this. Every shilling that comes in has a job before it gets spent.

50% restock  |  30% household  |  20% savings
Nonro: E giko mar chieng', till kod pesa e lwete duto en KSh 2,400.
KSh 1,200 odong' e kichni ne chuwo mar nyikine (unga, rech, magadi, mrenda, marge).
KSh 720 dhi e odhoge (seko, skul, sabuni, gik matin).
KSh 480 dhi e kepa mar kano. Inyalo ng'ado gik ma kuom dwe 3. Kuom dwe 3 ibiro ni yudo chuwo mang'eny na inyalo golo jiko mar ariyo kata ngeyo diriso.
Example: At the end of the day, your till and cash together total KSh 2,400. KSh 1,200 stays in the kitchen for tomorrow's stock. KSh 720 goes to the household. KSh 480 goes into a separate savings envelope. Do not touch this for 3 months. After 3 months you will have enough to buy a second jiko (stove) or repair the window frame.

Kanong'o ok en gima inyalo golo. En bumbo mar gik moromo amora ma biro. Kata KSh 100 pile ma okan en KSh 3,000 dwe achiel kendo KSh 36,000 higa. Ma en dho manyien, sign manyien, kata dwe adek mar seko ka koth owuok.
The savings is not optional. It is the seed of every future improvement. Even KSh 100 per day saved is KSh 3,000 per month and KSh 36,000 per year. That is a new door, a new sign, or three months of rent covered if the rains come.

Kwan mar M-Pesa mar Pile

M-Pesa Daily Tally

Otieno, kapok ininjo, namba adek mar nyaka riek. Ka ok gi rieko, gimoro osep kendo onego inon nyaka nino ma, ok nyikine. Every evening, before you sleep, three numbers must match. If they do not, something went wrong and you need to find it tonight, not tomorrow.

M-Pesa till total + cash in hand + credit given out = expected from sales
Nonro: Ikuondo nyagli 30. Nyagli achiel kepo ne KSh 130. Ma en KSh 3,900 duto ma otimo.
Wach mar M-Pesa till nyiso: KSh 2,100 oyudo nino.
Pesa e kepa mari: KSh 1,500.
Jopuo ariyo owiyi KSh 100 (iwa gi chulo kinde): KSh 200.
Duto: 2,100 + 1,500 + 200 = KSh 3,800.
Ma otimo: 3,900. Gima pok: KSh 100 ma geng'o. Nono nino: kichni mar awacho ma osetiek, nyagli ma ok owinj, kata till ma osewoyo.
Example: You served 30 plates. Average plate was KSh 130. So you should have KSh 3,900 total. M-Pesa till message says: KSh 2,100 received today. Cash in your tin: KSh 1,500. Two students owe you KSh 100 each (you let them pay later): KSh 200. Total: 2,100 + 1,500 + 200 = KSh 3,800. Expected: 3,900. Difference: KSh 100 short. Find it now.

Gima geng'o matin pile opogo gi loso ma owuok pile. Puo ok miyo hul. Ka utimo gik ma kuom ndalo 30, ubiro nono wiya ma oseloso pesa.
A small difference every day becomes a big loss every month. The math does not lie. If you do this for 30 days, you will find the leak.

Wa mar Piny Achiel

The One Page Summary

Printo ma. Tano e dho mar kichni. Print this. Tape it inside the kitchen door.

  1. Nengo mar chiemo nyaka podhi e 35 percent mar nengo mar chuwo. Food cost must stay under 35 percent of the selling price.

  4. Pogop sheleng' moromo amora: 50 chuwo, 30 oda, 20 kanong'o. Split every shilling: 50 stock, 30 household, 20 savings.
  5. Rik till, pesa kod chuwo mar kinde pile-pile e otieno. Match the till, the cash, and the credit every single evening.
