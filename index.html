Yot kamano nonro ma iwinjo

A few words for you to hear first

Beatrice is opening a window restaurant from her little house in Western Kenya. It is not a fancy place. It is a window, a counter, a stove, a kettle, and a mother who knows how to feed people.

Two big crowds walk past her gate every single day: the college students who need a hot plate before class, and the hospital workers who need a fast meal on a long shift. They will come back when the food is good and the price is fair. That is the whole business.

"Chakula maber, pile maber, thuolo maber. Ka iyie winjo, iyawo biro."
Good food, good welcome, good measure. When you hear about it, they will come.

The plan is simple: cook five or six things very well. Set up an M-Pesa till. Open WhatsApp. Hand out a few free plates to the nurses. Tell the students to spread the word. Within one month, the window will be known.

The Plan in Four Steps

Yo ariyo magi ka winjogo

1. Foundation

Week 1 — 2
  • Pick the name & paint a signboard
  • Set up M-Pesa Till (free for customers)
  • Print a small price card by the window
  • Test three recipes with neighbors

2. Launch

Week 3 — 4
  • Open WhatsApp Business with menu
  • Post food photos every morning on Status
  • Free plate to five nurses on day one
  • Student ambassador: two plates for two shares

3. Steady Crowd

Month 2 — 3
  • Same six dishes, same hours, every day
  • Delivery to the hospital gate for night shift
  • Sunday plate: rice & chicken for families
  • Track every sale in a small book

4. Grow

Month 4 onward
  • Add a second helper when the queue is long
  • Birthday plates for hospital staff
  • Chapati in bulk for road travelers
  • Save half of every extra shilling

How to Pay

Yot unyalo chulo

M-Pesa Till Number: to be printed on the window once set up
Cash: always welcome, always have small change ready
WhatsApp Order: send a message, pay on pickup, walk away happy

Tip: the till number is for customers, not for you. It is their number to send to, not for you to receive. That small detail is what makes the difference between a professional and a hobby.
